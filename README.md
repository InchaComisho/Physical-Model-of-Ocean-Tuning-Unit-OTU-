1. Abstract

This document defines the physical model of the Ocean Tuning Unit (OTU), a system designed to restore oceanic vertical circulation and enhance planetary cooling through deep-sea aeration.
The OTU integrates two key mechanisms:

1. Spiral-driven forced air injection (non-compressive transport)
2. Self-amplifying buoyancy-driven vertical convection (airlift effect)

Unlike conventional approaches that rely on high-pressure compression, the OTU utilizes geometric transport and natural physical laws to reduce energy requirements and achieve large-scale fluid circulation.



2. System Overview

The OTU operates as a coupled system of:

* A **Spiral Air Injection Pipe** (downward transport)
* A **Bubble Expansion Column** (upward energy generation)
* A **Tension-Stabilized Vertical Structure**

The system creates an artificial vertical convection loop:


Air Injection ↓ → Deep Release → Bubble Expansion ↑ → Water Entrainment ↑




3. Spiral-Driven Air Injection Model

3.1 Concept

Instead of compressing air to match deep-sea pressure, the OTU uses a **helical transport mechanism** (Archimedean-like spiral) to physically move air-water mixtures downward.

This converts the problem from:

* Pressure resistance → **Fluid transport**



3.2 Governing Principle

The volumetric transport rate is approximated by:

[
Q = A \cdot v
]

Where:

* ( Q ): volumetric flow rate
* ( A ): effective cross-sectional area of spiral cavity
* ( v ): axial transport velocity



3.3 Torque Requirement

The required torque is dominated by:

* Hydrostatic pressure gradient
* Viscous drag
* Multiphase flow resistance

[
\tau \propto \rho \cdot g \cdot h \cdot r
]

Where:

* ( \rho ): fluid density
* ( g ): gravitational acceleration
* ( h ): depth
* ( r ): spiral radius



3.4 Multiphase Flow Consideration

The transported medium is not pure air, but:

* Air + seawater mixture
* Intermittent bubble pockets

This reduces effective compression requirements and allows transport under lower energy conditions.



4. Bubble Expansion & Buoyancy Acceleration

4.1 Fundamental Law

Bubble expansion follows **Boyle’s Law**:

[
P_1 V_1 = P_2 V_2
]

As depth decreases:

* Pressure ↓
* Volume ↑



4.2 Buoyant Force

[
F_b = \rho_{water} \cdot g \cdot V_{bubble}
]

As bubble volume increases, buoyant force increases non-linearly.



4.3 Self-Amplifying Upward Flow

The rising bubbles create an **airlift effect**:

* Surrounding water is entrained upward
* Flow accelerates with decreasing depth

This creates a **positive feedback loop**:


Bubble expansion → Increased buoyancy → Faster ascent → More entrainment




4.4 Transition from Nanobubbles

Initial release:

* Nanobubbles (high surface area, slow rise)

During ascent:

* Coalescence → Larger bubbles
* Transition to cm-scale bubbles near surface



5. Vertical Circulation Model

5.1 Induced Flow

The system generates a vertical circulation column:

* Upward flow (bubble-driven)
* Downward compensation flow (surrounding water)



5.2 Approximate Velocity

[
v_{up} \approx \sqrt{\frac{2 \cdot \Delta \rho}{\rho} \cdot g \cdot h}
]

Where:

* ( \Delta \rho ): density difference due to gas content



5.3 Environmental Impact

The induced circulation enables:

* Nutrient upwelling
* Oxygen distribution
* CO₂ fixation enhancement



6. Structural Mechanics (Tension-Stabilized Pipe)

6.1 Design Concept

The vertical pipe is not rigid but **tension-stabilized**:

* Top: buoyant float
* Bottom: ballast weight



6.2 Stability Model

[
T = W - B
]

Where:

* ( T ): tension

* ( B ): buoyant force

Proper tuning ensures vertical alignment under ocean currents.
